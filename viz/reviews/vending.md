# vending — visualization review

## What the program does

`vending.ev` is an **autonomous limit cycle**. State is mixed: `mode ∈ {Idle, Coining, Vending}` (enum), `balance ∈ Int`, `dispensed ∈ Bool`. There is no input — the machine auto-inserts a coin each tick until it can vend. The transition is a fixed loop: `Idle → Coining → (Coining while balance<3) → Vending → Idle`, with `balance` ramping 0→1→2→3 during Coining, resetting to 0 on the Idle/Vending edges, and `dispensed` pulsing true for exactly the one tick after Vending. The reachable set is 7 distinct states forming a single period-6 cycle plus the initial bootstrap state. The interesting structure is purely the **shape and period of that cycle** and the phase relationship between the three carried variables.

## Ranked best → worst

1. **fixedpoint_map** — The single best fit. Title literally announces "1 cycle(s) (period [6])" and draws the cycle as a directed closed loop in (balance × mode): balance ramps along the Coining row, jumps up to Vending at balance=3, then the long diagonal arrow back to Idle/balance=0. It both *detects* and *renders* exactly the limit cycle that is the program's whole point. Faithful and diagnostic.
2. **timing_diagram** — Best for understanding the *temporal phase relationship*. 40 ticks make the periodicity unmistakable: sawtooth balance, the enum lane stepping Idle→Coining→Vending, and the `dispensed` digital pulse firing one tick after each Vending. Shows all three variables in their true lockstep. The most readable "what actually happens over time" view.
3. **time_series** — Same content as timing_diagram over 8 ticks (one period). The balance trapezoid, mode step ladder, and the single dispensed pulse at tick 6 are all correct and clear. Slightly redundant with timing_diagram but stands alone well.
4. **state_graph** — Faithful node-link of the 7-state ring: the (1,2,3 Coining) chain → (3,Vending) → (0,Idle) → (0,Coining) closing the loop, colored by mode, sized by balance. Reads as a genuine cycle. Good, just visually looser than fixedpoint_map.
5. **phase_portrait** — Correct topology (7 states, 7 transitions, the balance ramp + Vending spike + diagonal return) but the node jitter pushes "Idle/balance 0" off to -0.1 and duplicates the Coining row visually. The right idea, executed more noisily than fixedpoint_map which plots the same axes cleanly.
6. **chord_diagram** — Compact and honest: 3 mode nodes, thick blue arcs Idle→Coining→Vending, and the orange (dispensed=true) arc on the Vending→Idle return. Correctly encodes that dispensing happens only on the reset edge. Nice secondary summary.
7. **parallel_coords** — Surprisingly legible for 3 axes (balance, mode, dispensed): the green Vending line ties balance=3 to dispensed, the blue Idle line sits at balance=0. The mode axis labels (Idle/Coining/Vending) help. Shows the per-state correlations, not the dynamics.
8. **transition_matrix** — Dense 14×14 from/to grid with a faithful but hard-to-read scatter of single yellow cells along the cycle. All the information is there; the labels are microscopic. Correct, low ergonomics.
9. **reachability_tree** — A straight vertical chain of all 7 states (it's a cycle, so BFS first-discovery is linear). Accurate but says nothing the state_graph doesn't, and hides that the last node loops back.
10. **occupancy_heatmap** — Correct in principle (bright cells exactly on the visited (balance,mode) pairs), but the cell-vs-tick-mark misalignment makes the bars look like they float between rows. Faithful data, sloppy rendering.
11. **scatter_matrix** — The 3×3 pairs do show balance∈{0,1,2,3}, mode∈3 values, dispensed mostly false. But with 8 points and two categorical axes it's mostly sparse dots and bar histograms; little insight into the cyclic dynamics.
12. **orbit_scatter** — Jittered cloud of the orbit in (balance × mode); the seed ring sits at Idle and the lone orange (dispensed=true) point is at Idle/balance≈0. The jitter destroys the loop structure that phase_portrait/fixedpoint_map preserve. Weakly useful.
13. **basin_map** — Technically correct (one basin, the cycle, attractor star) but trivially so: an autonomous machine has exactly one basin, so the 18-seed grid is just a uniform field of identical blue squares. No information beyond "it all converges to the one cycle."
14. **cobweb** — Degenerate. Holding mode/dispensed constant collapses the balance map to a flat line at y=0 (because outside Coining, balance resets to 0), so `x_{n+1}=f(x_n)` is just dots on the x-axis and the orbit is a single red point at origin. Misleading: implies balance is a fixed point at 0, hiding the actual ramp.
15. **morse_graph** — Misleading condensation. It correctly finds one nontrivial SCC ("cycle ×6") but renders it as a single generic blob with a truncated label, throwing away all the cycle's internal order that makes this program interesting. Less informative than just listing the states.
16. **nullcline_field** — Clean N/A: only one numeric axis (balance) and no suitable facet, so a nullcline (sign of d(var)) can't be drawn. Correct to bail; produces no false picture, but contributes nothing.

## Verdict

- **Keepers:** `fixedpoint_map` (detects + draws the period-6 limit cycle — the program's essence), `timing_diagram` (the temporal phase relationship of all three variables), and `state_graph`/`chord_diagram` as the cleanest topology summaries.
- **Drop for this program:** `cobweb` (degenerate, actively misleading about balance), `morse_graph` (condenses the one interesting cycle into a featureless blob), `basin_map` (one trivial basin), and `nullcline_field` (correctly N/A). `orbit_scatter` and `occupancy_heatmap` are weak due to jitter/alignment rendering issues.
- **Notable finding:** the cobweb plot is a genuine trap here — it shows balance as a fixed point pinned at 0, which is the *opposite* of the truth (balance ramps 0→3 every cycle). The artifact arises because cobweb holds the enum mode constant, and outside Coining the balance update is the reset branch. Any cobweb of a variable whose update depends on a held-constant enum companion will mislead. Conversely, `fixedpoint_map` is the standout: it's the only view that both *names* the period and *draws the closed orbit* in one picture.
